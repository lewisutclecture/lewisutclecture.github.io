Recorded Lectures

Audio recordings of our past lectures.
(Update: the code update to HTML 5 which enabled play on i-pads may not work on Chrome at this time. If not, please use something other than Chrome to hear the lectures)

2023 Lecture: Lecturer: Dr. Holly Ordway of the Word on Fire Institute. The lecture title is “Tolkien's and Lewis's Friendship”

 

2022 Lecture: Lecturer: David C. Downing, Co-Director of the Marion E. Wade Center at Wheaton College in Illinois. The lecture title is “The Most Reluctant Convert”

 

2019 Lecture: Lecturer: Dr. Donald T. Williams, R. A. Forrest Scholar and Professor of English at Toccoa Falls College.  The lecture title is “Ludicrous, Lacking, or Logical? The Validity of Lewis’s Trilemma.”

 

2018 Lecture: Lecturer: Dr. Justin Dyer, Associate Professor of Political Science, University of Missouri. He and a colleague have recently published CS Lewis on Politics and Natural Law (Cambridge, 2016), reviewed very favorably here in the NY Times.

2017 Lecture: Lecturer: Dr. John Stackhouse, The Samuel J. Mikolaski Professor of Religious Studies and Dean of Faculty Development, Crandall University in Moncton, New Brunswick.

2016 Lecture: Dr. Joseph Loconte, PhD, Associate Professor of History at The King’s College in New York City; Lecture topic: “J.R.R. Tolkien and C.S. Lewis: Finding Hope in the Cataclysm of War” (audio missing)

2015 33rd Lecture: Michael Ward, DPhil., Senior Research Fellow at Blackfriars Hall, Oxford.
